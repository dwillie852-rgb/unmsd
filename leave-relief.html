Staff care programme

A dedicated pathway for spouses and immediate family members to request time at home for doctors deployed in conflict, displacement, outbreak, or hardship missions without leaving patients uncovered.

Why this exists

Rest is not separate from medical continuity. It is part of it.

Field doctors carry high caseloads, security pressure, family separation, and repeated exposure to medical distress. The leave relief programme gives families a formal way to request home leave while the operations team protects the clinic, referral chain, and patients who still need care.

Leave is approved only when a replacement plan exists. If coverage cannot be secured for the requested window, the applicant receives revised options or a refund of unapplied programme fees.

Process

Four checks before a doctor travels home.

01

Family request

The spouse or immediate family member submits the doctor, station, role, preferred window, and contact details.

02

Mission review

Operations confirms caseload, security movement, active referrals, pharmacy status, and medical leadership sign-off.

03

Replacement match

A clinician with the right specialty and field profile is contracted, briefed, and scheduled for handover.

04

Leave clearance

Travel is cleared when patient notes, referral lists, duty rosters, and security handover are complete.

Who can apply

Built for families, verified by operations.

  • Spouse, partner, parent, adult child, or designated emergency contact.
  • Doctor must be active on a Doctors Without Border field assignment.
  • Requested leave must be specific enough for replacement planning.
  • Mission desk must confirm that the doctor can travel safely during the requested period.

What you need

Documents and details.

  • Applicant identity and relationship to the doctor
  • Doctor's full name, role, and current field station
  • Preferred leave dates and minimum acceptable window
  • Reliable contact details for urgent operations follow-up

Questions

Common leave relief questions.

Can leave be declined?

Yes. If movement is unsafe, caseload is unstable, or no qualified replacement is available, operations will offer revised dates or refund unapplied fees.

Is the fee a donation?

No. It is an operational leave relief cost assigned to replacement staffing, handover, travel coordination, and reserve.

Who approves the leave?

The field medical lead, security desk, and operations desk all need to clear the leave window before travel is confirmed.

What happens after submission?

The applicant receives a review reference. Operations then validates the mission details and contacts the applicant for next steps.
